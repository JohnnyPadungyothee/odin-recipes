Tonkotsu Ramen Bowl - The Most Satisfying Meal

This Tonkotsu Ramen Bowl is one of the most popular variations of ramen. Tonkotsu is a fatty pork broth that is creamier and richer than most other ramen broths. It is traditionally served with a soft-boiled egg and charred slices of pork belly.

Ingredients

  • 3 pounds pig trotters
  • 2 pounds chicken backs
  • 2 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion
  • 12 garlic cloves
  • 1 pound slab pork fat back
  • 2 eggs
  • Noodles

Steps

  1. Boil off the residue in the pig trotters in a pot of boiling water
  2. Bring water to a boil and add pig trotters and chicken backs
  3. Let simmer for 12 hours
  4. Slice the pork fat back into manageable pieces
  5. Saute onions and garlic in heated pan with vegetable oil
  6. Sear slices of pork fat back
  7. Boil eggs for 6 minutes then immediately place them in an ice bath after removing them from the boiling water
  8. Boil the noodles to al dente
  9. Once the soup is complete, add the soup to a bowl
  10. Place the noodles into the bowl, along with the soft-boiled eggs and seared pork fat back
